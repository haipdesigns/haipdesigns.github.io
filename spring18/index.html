Haip focuses on the individuality of the garments and thus the wearer through thoughtful research that translates into handcrafted, minimal and playful garments. As well as using aspects of environmentally conscious design like choosing natural fibers and designing with waste concious patterns.

SPRING 2018 (PREVIEW)

Questioning the limits of the role of designer, this system uses personal connection translated into knit techniques. This connection does not rely on the skill set of the individuals but uses what is revealed through community to propel it. This knitwear reincorporates memory into a personal knit that is further enriched by the use of T-Shirts for the base material.

Example of an initial experiment in testing the openness of design to individuals who are not in the design field.

Using my developed T-Shirt yarn I continued using community engagements to propel my design techniques. This swatch shows how my interaction with Jerome and his favorite smell was developed into a knit sample.

Key to my process is the connections that are formed through the creation of personal knit techniques and the way that people are gathering and meeting as a result.

A major breakthrough in my techniques is the material development of creating and using yarn from discarded tshirts from a variety of individuals.
